# Firing a Customer

Use this when a customer’s behavior, property conditions, or payment issues make it unsafe or unsustainable to keep them on the route.

## Before you send it
- Confirm the decision with your route manager or owner.
- Document the missed payments, unsafe dog behavior, gate/access issues, or abusive communication.
- Keep the message short, factual, and professional.

## Message structure
1. State that service will end.
2. Give the brief reason.
3. Provide the final service date or stop date.
4. Close professionally.

## Common reasons
- Repeated non-payment for recurring yard clean-up.
- Aggressive or uncontrolled dogs on the property.
- Locked gates or blocked access after multiple notices.
- Continual disrespect toward crew members.

## Example line
"Because we have not been able to safely and reliably service your yard, we will be ending service effective [date]. Thank you for understanding."
