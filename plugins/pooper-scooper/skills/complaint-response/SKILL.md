# Complaint Response

Use this skill when a customer is upset about a missed pick-up, a messy yard, a billing mix-up, or a broken service promise.

## What to do first
- Acknowledge the issue clearly.
- Accept responsibility if the route or service missed the mark.
- Explain what you will do to fix it.

## Suggested structure
1. Open with empathy and a short apology.
2. Summarize the problem in a yard-specific way.
3. State what you will do to correct it and when.
4. Offer a simple next step or check-in.

## Tone
- Calm, human, and solution-focused.
- Avoid blame and long justifications.
- Mention the impact on the customer’s yard and schedule.

## Sample wording
"I’m sorry we missed your yard on Tuesday. I know that leaves dog waste sitting there longer than it should, and that’s not the experience we want to provide. I’ll come by tomorrow to finish the clean-up and make sure your next scheduled service happens as planned."
