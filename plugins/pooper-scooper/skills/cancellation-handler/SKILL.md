# Cancellation Handler

Use this skill when a customer says they want to cancel, pause, or reduce recurring pet waste pickup service.

## When to use it
- A customer wants to stop weekly or biweekly yard cleanup.
- They say the price is too high, the service is too frequent, or they want to switch to DIY.
- You want to preserve the route without sounding desperate.

## What to ask
- "Is the issue the price, the schedule, or the way we handled your yard?"
- "Would a temporary pause or a switch to every-other-week help?"
- "Do you still want the same pickup day if we keep going?"

## Recommended approach
1. Say you understand and avoid arguing.
2. Ask one calm, specific question to learn the real reason.
3. Offer a route-friendly retention option, such as a seasonal pause, a frequency change, or a lower-cost maintenance visit.
4. If they still want to leave, confirm the final date and keep it clean.

## Language that works
- "I get it — no one wants waste piling up in their yard."
- "If the budget is tight, we can move you to every other week until things settle down."
- "If you want to stop, I’ll make sure we finish this month on the right note."

## Good closing line
"If you'd like, we can pause service for a while or switch to a lighter schedule while you decide. Otherwise, we can close your account cleanly effective [date]."
